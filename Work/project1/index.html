Artist Statement

The tessellation process of designing a singular modular and figuring out a common joint was a little challenging. My main priority for these exercises was to follow the detailed instructions with careful insight so that everything would work well together as far as the pieces went. Exercise one was a nice introduction to learning how to fold origami. Also developing patience and taking my time to make the final result. Exercise 2 brought one challenge of making sure we were able to fulfill the snake option A. My set of origami did not fit the other groups pieces, but eventually I found way to make it work. Exercise 3 I acquired many trial and error modulars. I ended up making a three-dimensional origami hexagon which nicely demonstrated fasteners and folds. My group also came up with great ideas of different fasteners which helped us conclude our final project. Exercise 4 was fun to play with in illustrator, as it has been a primary platform to create art. This exercise was the first time I was able to collaborate with a group. Are designs all unique and colorful. We also found separate ways to play around with shapes we developed, and they made a playful design overall.

Illustrator Cut Designs

Artist Statment: The groups design overall were all unique and ceative. The group wanted to try out the .125 fastner, but luckly before the cutting process we noticed that all of our fastners were different. We all manipulated the fastner with differebt widths and lengths but kept at the center .125. We ended creating a final fastner that eventually would work for the cutting tests.

Exercise 2

Goldilocks Laser tests: Initial Designs tests for Fasteners

Artist Statment: The groups designs in excercise 2 were focused around a nature theme. Since these were more organic shaped pieces we had to calculate the fastners to work around the design were they wouldnt interfere being cut through with laser. We test cutted with a wood cut and a acrylic cut. The wood cut fastner of.125 was snug and little tight and we knew we had to make an adjustment there. With the acrylic, we didnt make any adjustment to .125 fastner but that caused connecting pieces to have a minor loose fit. In the end we chose to work with the acrylic material for our final pieces and adjusted the fastner once more to make the pices fit. That being only adjusting the fastners end points to be pushed inward once rather than twice; and length to be a little longer (H:.04) (W:.125).

Artist Statement

Digital Video Diaries (DVD) is a group collaboration between a team founded in ART 103 at San Jose State University. My Team consisted of four members: Van, Mandy, Alyssa, and myself Robert. The main goal of this project was to create a more aesthetic and artistic visual diary that highlighted emotions of an individual. A colorful visual record that can help users see how they are feeling on a daily basis. We hoped DVD could be a positive exercise and also a wonderful way to create art at the same time. I worked on the mood Innerpace with transparent colored acrylic pieces. I personally tried to practice mindfulness and wanted to portray actions to take into five pieces. These five representations are drinking water, being in flow with life, relaxing, finding peace through meditation, and developing a healthy mindset.

Successful/Future Changes

One successful goal was assembling the pieces together and having a light source shine through them to make a unique projection of the transparent acrylic pieces. I thought the group worked overall really well together. We demonstrated effective communication, met the deadlines, and worked hard to see our vision come to life. I think the only minor issue to fix for the future is getting our fasteners to be either a little tighter or just come up woth a whole new one. Our final fastener worked but we did have a few pieces that were looser than others due to designs being closer to the fastener.
