Final Project

Artist Statement

As a student in college there are many events that affect our daily lives and our mental health. From assigned homework, bills, jobs, relationships, etc. We can tend to create an unorganized motive to balance these tasks and successfully getting them done. I will create experiments that cultivate awareness of the present moment and clear the mind of other thoughts, to help with decision making to complete these tasks. I will be conducting a few Meditation Exercises to see how it can reduce my stress levels, thus allowing me to organize my thoughts and make decisions.

For the final project I chose to upgrade my inclosure. I lasered cutted a wooden box, a plexie glass top,painted one side with acrylic, and lastly added my buttons and LEDs. Successfully I was able to reduce my mental stress from meditating between 10-20 minutes which occurred during the second meditation that I did. Also, I realized I am able to adjust my stress and decision making into getting tasks completed due the meditation exercises as they are helpful tools. The GSR is a helpful tool to interact with people. The GSR test allows to visually see results and record them for future complications with student and personal life tasks. Also it is a tool that can help dictate stress in a visual way or even conduct a variety of different experiments that can help figure out why we feel the way we do in our daily lives. However, the GSR may not always be right in terms of dictating stress depending on the person where their personal stress levels may affect results. I think it has potential for a decision technique. Depending on what you want to experiment with like different meditations or music choices, and factoring coding into manipulating how the GSR responds to your liking; I think the visual graph in Arduino was the biggest reliable source that helped make my decisions. Again, it allowed me to see how my body was reacting to my exercises that I tried and helped me make decisions in the end.
